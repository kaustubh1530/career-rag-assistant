IT Intern – Software Engineering Track 
Job Description 
The PCRB IT internship will allow you to apply skills and knowledge you have learned in school, 
while contributing to company projects and playing a key role within the IT department. Our 
company will provide you hands-on experience to fit your career objectives, evolve and 
complement your academic studies and help grow your knowledge base. The paid internship 
runs for approximately 7-10 weeks throughout the summer beginning in early June through end 
of August. 
Essential Responsibilities: 
• Create root cause analysis documents for minor issues fixed recently for key apps in 
PCRB by doing reverse engineering of code base 
• Support and understand standard software life cycle process for Waterfall and Agile 
methodology 
• Participate and assist in testing applications (smoke testing and functional testing 
aspects) 
• Be able to learn and utilize basic tools for GIT usage to learn code repository basic 
details 
• Creating technical documentation on new processes, as well as additions and 
enhancements to already established documentation 
• Assist the IT Infrastructure team in addressing system and technology alerts or 
interruptions of service 
• Gain knowledge in troubleshooting, diagnosing, resolving, and communicating solutions 
to end user’s IT problems 
• Assist in configuring and deploying new user equipment (Laptops, Docking Stations, 
Screens, etc.) 
• Utilize research, problem solving and analytical skills to support end-users and 
contribute to projects 
• Gain exposure to a range of information technology areas, such as applications, systems 
architecture, network systems and infrastructure 
• Perform other miscellaneous duties as requested  
Requirements: 
Education: Currently pursuing a B.S. in Information Systems/Technology, Computer Science, or 
related field. 
Experience: No previous experience required, but candidates must express willingness and 
aptitude to learn new skills quickly and show earnest ability to work both independently and 
with instruction 
Technical Skills: 
• C# or VB .net code beneficial, but not required 
• Basic SQL skills beneficial, but not required 
Core Skills Required: 
• Ability to articulate IT’s role in enabling and supporting innovation, competitive 
differentiation, and business process/workflow efficiencies 
• Excellent oral and written English communication, interpersonal, presentation and 
planning skills at a macro levels. Strong decision-making skills. 
• Intermediate to advanced Microsoft Office proficiency required to analyze and present 
information (Excel, Word, Access and PowerPoint). 
• Fundamental knowledge of desktop computer hardware, operating systems and 
computer networks
